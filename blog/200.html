The Era of Mass Surveillance

Mass surveillance is the surveillance of the population of an entire nation or nations. Mass surveillance is carried out by governments and corporations. Mass surveillance has been justified by governments to fight against terrorism and protect national security. Researchers, security experts and concerned citizens have brought to light the abuse of power that comes with mass surveillance.

Mass Surveillance is a Global Issue

In 2013, Edward Snowden a former NSA and CIA contractor released classified information about the NSA’s programs that collected and intercepted information about everyone online and through their phones at large scales

Your metadata knows you better than anyone else.

Metadata is the identifiable information contained about the senders and receivers within every call, message, etc. Metadata can be used to build a profile about you.
The NSA programs exposed by Edward Snowden shows how they collected all metadata from every major internet company.
